# Key Resources to Fight Wildfires:

## Personnel & Specialized Teams:

- `Hotshot Crews`: Highly trained, hand crews that work in steep, rugged terrain.
- `Incident Management Teams (IMTs)`: Professionals managing large-scale, complex incidents.
- `Structural Firefighters`: Specialized in protecting homes.
- `Aerial Resources` (Aerial Firefighting):
- `Airtankers/Scoopers`: Drop water and fire retardant to slow fire spread.
- `Helicopters`: Used for water drops, transporting crews, and scouting.
- `Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS/Drones)`: Used for surveillance and mapping.
- `Ground Equipment` & Vehicles:
- `Wildland Fire Engines`: All-terrain engines designed for off-road water pumping.
- `Bulldozers`: Used to create fire breaks (firelines) by clearing vegetation.
- `Water Tenders`: Large trucks that transport water to refill engines.
- `Hand Tools`: Pulaskis, shovels, fire flappers, and chainsaws.
---
## Support & Technology:
- `Fire Retardant`: Chemical mixtures to slow fire spread.
- `Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)`: Fire-resistant clothing (Nomex), hard hats, goggles, and fire shelters.
- `Predictive Services`: Fire weather forecasting to anticipate fire movement.
- `Logistics`: Catering units, shower units, and communication systems.

## Prevention Resources (Pre-Fire):
`Defensible Space`: Creating a 30-to-100-foot buffer of non-flammable space around structures.
`Fuel Reduction`: Thinning forests and removing ladder fuels to reduce intensity.

## Governmental/Financial Aid:
`Fire Management Assistance Grants (FMAG)`: Federal grants for states, local, and tribal governments.
